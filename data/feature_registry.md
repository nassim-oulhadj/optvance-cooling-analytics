# feature_registry.md, Engineered Feature Documentation

Source: `data/cooling_telemetry_doha_dc1.csv` → `data/features_engineered.csv`
Generated by: `notebooks/02_feature_engineering.ipynb` (Day 3)

59 features engineered across 6 categories. 95 rows (0.27% of the year) were
dropped due to lookback requirements, the 24-hour rolling window (96 steps) is
the binding constraint, since it absorbs every shorter lookback (including
`lag_32`) inside the same window. No anomaly rows were lost.

## 5. Time Features (8)

| Feature | Formula | Business Justification |
|---|---|---|
| `hour_of_day` | `timestamp.dt.hour` | Raw hour (0–23), lets tree models split directly on time-of-day without an encoding step |
| `day_of_week` | `timestamp.dt.dayofweek` | Raw weekday (0–6), operational load patterns can differ between weekdays and weekends |
| `month_num` | `timestamp.dt.month` | Raw month (1–12), captures seasonal regime directly for tree-based splits |
| `is_weekend` | `day_of_week.isin([5,6])` | Binary flag, coarser, more robust weekend indicator than the raw weekday integer |
| `sin_hour` | `sin(2π·hour/24)` | Cyclical encoding, makes hour 23 and hour 0 adjacent in feature space, fixing the discontinuity a raw integer creates at midnight |
| `cos_hour` | `cos(2π·hour/24)` | Cyclical encoding, paired with `sin_hour` to fully recover the hour's position on the 24h circle |
| `sin_month` | `sin(2π·month/12)` | Cyclical encoding, makes December and January adjacent, matching the dataset's actual seasonal continuity |
| `cos_month` | `cos(2π·month/12)` | Cyclical encoding, paired with `sin_month` for the month's position on the 12-month circle |

## 6. Interaction Features (3)

| Feature | Formula | Business Justification |
|---|---|---|
| `cop_x_outdoor_temp` | `chiller_cop * outdoor_temp_c` | Thermal stress proxy, the same COP value means something different in 45°C heat vs. 20°C cool; the product captures that the combination, not either term alone, indicates real stress |
| `load_to_flow_ratio` | `it_load_mw / pump_flow_rate_ls` | Load-to-flow ratio, high load with low flow is a materially worse operating point than either factor independently suggests, since the system can't move enough water to carry the heat away |
| `approach_x_humidity` | `cooling_tower_approach_c * outdoor_humidity_pct` | Fouling risk index, humidity affects how hard the cooling tower must work to reject heat for a given approach temperature; the product approximates combined scaling/fouling risk |

## Dropped Rows (NaN Handling)

95 rows are dropped, all from the start of January 2025. The 24-hour rolling
window (96 steps) is the binding constraint: it requires 95 prior rows before
its first valid value, and that requirement fully absorbs every shorter
lookback in the feature set, including `lag_32`'s 32-row requirement.

Anomaly coverage of the drop, broken out by population: 0 of the 8 structured
events are affected, since the anomaly scheduler deliberately avoids the first
5 and last 10 days of the year, so none can ever land in this window. The 943
background rows have no such avoidance, since they're sampled uniformly across
the entire remaining-normal pool, so 4 of them fall in the first 95 rows purely
by chance and are dropped along with the rest of that window. Net result: 1,062
of 1,066 total ground-truth anomaly rows (99.6%) survive into
`features_engineered.csv`, that's 123 of 123 structured and 939 of 943
background.
